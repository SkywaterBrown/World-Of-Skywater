The radio hums between stations.
Not silence, but the shape of silence—
white noise wearing a collar of static,
pretending to be a voice.

I have grown accustomed to the hum.
It fills the room when words fail,
a mechanical tide that asks nothing
and gives nothing back.

Sometimes, in the static, I think I hear
your name. But it is only interference—
a truck passing, a phone searching for signal,
the ghost of a conversation I never had.
