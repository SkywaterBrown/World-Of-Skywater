[A photograph of still water, mirroring clouds that are not there.]

The lake does not distinguish between sky and shore. It reflects what passes overhead and what falls at its edge with equal indifference. In the early morning, before the wind arrives, the surface becomes a second sky—darker, more forgiving, less temporary.

I took this photograph standing at the water's edge with my boots sinking slightly into mud. The exposure was long enough to smooth the last ripples, short enough to keep the clouds from drifting. The result is a kind of quiet that cameras can record but never reproduce.
